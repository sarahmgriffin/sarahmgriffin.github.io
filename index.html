Sarah M. Griffin

Researcher of late medieval visual culture: Museums, manuscripts, diagrams, technology, time, and the use of digital tools for object-based teaching.


Education

  • DPhil, History of Art, University of Oxford , Kellogg College (2014–18). Funded by an AHRC Studentship, working on the diagrams of Opicinus de Canistris.
  • MA, History of Art, Courtauld Institute of Art (2012–13). Course: ‘Making and Meaning of Art in the Middle Ages' with Prof. John Lowden.
  • BA and MA (Cantab), History of Art, University of Cambridge, Churchill College (2009–12).

Employment

  • The Warburg Institute, Frances A. Yates Long-Term Fellow (September, 2021)
  • Winchester College, Assistant Curator (2019–21)
  • Oxford Internet Institute, University of Oxford, Postdoctoral Research assistant for Cabinet [Link], an online database of Oxford University's collections for the purpose of undergraduate teaching (2016–19)
  • Ashmolean Museum, Junior Teaching Fellow and co-convener for Krasis, an award-winning teaching initiative run by the Mellon-funded Oxford University Engagement Partnership (2017–19)
  • Coventry City of Culture Trust and Oxford University, Coventry/Oxford. Researcher for 'Royal Women of Influence in Medieval Coventry' [Link] (2018)
  • Metropolitan Museum of Art, New York. Graduate intern in the Medieval Department (June–August, 2013)
  • Duncan H McLaren Ltd Art Brokers, London. Catalogued the archive of Sir Simon Marsden [Link] (2012)

Awards and Grants

  • St Andrews Institute of Medieval Studies essay prize, winner of 2020 award for 'Time Unfolded: A Late Medieval Concertina Calendar in the Staatsbibliothek zu Berlin (Libr. pict. A 92)'.
  • Neil Ker Memorial Fund (2020–21). Awarded for the project ‘A Verbal/Visual Catalogue of Medieval Concertina-Fold Almanacs’ with Megan McNamee.
  • Staatsbibliothek zu Berlin (July–August, 2019). Research Grant (2 months) awarded by the Stiftung Preussischer Kulturbesitz for the proposed project: 'Hours of Daylight, Hours of Darkness: Diagrams of Time in a Late Medieval Almanac (Libr. pict. A 92)'.
  • Fondazione Giorgio Cini and TORCH (Oxford Humanities), Venice (April, 2017). Research project on horological devices, wall paintings in fourteenth- and fifteenth-century Veneto and the printed book of Regiomontanus.
  • Huntington Library, California (August, 2016). Dibner Fellowship in the History of Science and Technology for one-month project: ‘Quantifying time and theology in the medieval diagrams of HM 83 and HM 1104,’ studying the impact of the shift from qualitative and quantitative thinking in the exact sciences on devotional image making in northern-Italian manuscripts.
  • Associate Fellowship of the Higher Education Academy (January, 2017), following the submission of a teaching portfolio on object-based pedagogy.
  • AHRC Studentship for doctoral research (2014–17).
